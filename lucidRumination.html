01

Overview

Lucid Rumination is a first-person psychological horror roguelite developed as a junior-year capstone project at the University of California, Irvine, in collaboration with a small team under the name Jiji Games.

The project focused on systems-driven design and full-cycle Unity development, with an emphasis on building cohesive, interdependent gameplay systems within a constrained production timeline.

The game places players in the role of Miki, a coma patient undergoing experimental therapy, navigating procedurally generated environments shaped by memory, trauma, and psychological distortion.

The experience blends melee-focused combat, roguelite progression, and narrative-driven design to create a systemically rich and thematically unified gameplay loop.

04

Gameplay Systems

Roguelite Loop & Progression

The core gameplay loop revolves around repeated runs through procedurally generated rooms, where players prepare in a central hub, engage in combat encounters, collect upgrades, and progress toward boss encounters. Death resets most progress, but players retain selected resources and unlocks, reinforcing long-term progression and replayability.

Memory Integrity System

A central mechanic unique to the project — weapons degrade over time without numerical indicators. Instead, deterioration is communicated visually and audibly, encouraging players to adapt dynamically and reinforcing the game's psychological themes of memory loss and instability.

Inventory & Weapon Systems

The game features a grid-based inventory system inspired by Resident Evil, combined with a weapon merging system that allows players to create temporary, run-specific builds. Players must strategically choose which items to retain after death, emphasizing decision-making and loss as core design pillars.

Companion & Boon System

Inspired by Hades, players encounter companions during runs who provide boons, upgrades, and narrative context. These relationships expand gameplay possibilities while strengthening emotional and narrative engagement.

Combat & Movement

Combat is animation-driven, focusing on melee interactions with precise hitbox alignment and root motion. The system emphasizes responsiveness and physicality, drawing inspiration from titles like Cyberpunk 2077 and Dead Cells.

05

Technical Implementation

Modular Enemy AI

Enemy behavior was designed using a modular architecture, separating systems into perception, locomotion, and attack components, all governed by a central "brain" using finite state machines. This approach allowed for rapid iteration and scalability as design requirements evolved.

Procedural Environment Design

Levels are constructed from modular room templates, enabling procedural generation while maintaining strong thematic consistency. Environmental design evolves alongside narrative progression, transitioning from clinical spaces to abstract psychological environments.

Animation Pipeline

All player and enemy actions are driven by animations created in Blender, utilizing root motion and carefully aligned hitboxes to ensure gameplay clarity and responsiveness.

Performance & Architecture

The project utilizes a single-entry system architecture, where a central game object initializes all core managers and systems. Combined with object pooling techniques, this approach improves performance, reduces overhead, and maintains a clean, scalable codebase.

06

Production

Development was organized using Scrum methodologies, with weekly sprints managed through Atlassian tools. Due to limited team availability, the project required continuous scope adjustments.

To maintain quality, scope was strategically reduced while prioritizing core systems and polish, demonstrating practical trade-offs between time, scope, and quality constraints.

07

Key Takeaways

This project served as a significant step up in scope from prior game jam and prototype work, providing hands-on experience in:

  • Designing and integrating complex, interdependent systems
  • Managing scope and production constraints in a team environment
  • Building scalable technical architecture in Unity
  • Aligning game mechanics with narrative and thematic intent
